Land Survey

Detailed examination and mapping of a piece of land to determine its boundaries, features, and characteristics. It involves the use of specialized surveying equipment and techniques to accurately measure and document various aspects of the land.

Cadastral Survey

Specialized type of land survey that focuses on accurately mapping and delineating land ownership boundaries and related information. It involves the identification, measurement, and documentation of parcels of land, including their boundaries, dimensions, and ownership details. Cadastral surveys play a crucial role in land administration, property registration, land development, and land management.

Topographical Survey

type of land survey that focuses on mapping the physical features and characteristics of a piece of land. It provides detailed information about the elevation, contours, natural and man-made features, and other relevant details of the surveyed area. Topographic surveys are commonly conducted for engineering, architectural, planning, and land development purposes.

Static Survey

refer to a surveying technique or method that involves stationary measurements or observations taken at specific points on the land. This method is often used in geodetic surveys or high-precision surveys where precise positioning and measurements are required.

Change of User

process where the designated or permitted use of a property or land is modified or altered. It typically involves obtaining the necessary approvals, permits, or licenses to change the existing purpose or activities conducted on the property.

GIS Mapping

process of creating, analyzing, and visualizing geospatial data on digital maps. It involves the use of specialized software and techniques to collect, store, manipulate, and display geographic information.

Extension of Lease

refers to a process where the duration of an existing lease agreement is prolonged beyond its original expiration date. It allows the tenant to continue occupying the leased property for an additional period of time, subject to the terms and conditions negotiated between the landlord and the tenant.

Due Dilligence

comprehensive and systematic investigation or examination conducted by individuals or organizations before entering into a business transaction or making an investment. It is a process of gathering relevant information, analyzing risks and opportunities, and assessing the overall viability and value of the subject matter under consideration.

Sectional Property Survey

specific type of land survey conducted for properties that are governed by sectional title laws or regulations. Sectional title is a form of property ownership where individual units or sections within a larger property, such as apartments, townhouses, or commercial units, are owned by different individuals or entities.

Title Processing

refers to the administrative and legal steps involved in transferring the ownership of a property from one party to another. It encompasses various tasks and procedures aimed at ensuring a smooth and lawful transfer of title, establishing the new owner's legal rights and responsibilities.
